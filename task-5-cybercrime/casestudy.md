##Cybercrime Case Study: UPI Fraud

UPI fraud is a common cybercrime in India where scammers trick people into transferring money by misusing digital payment systems. In this type of fraud, criminals often contact victims through phone calls, messages, or fake links.

The fraud usually begins when a scammer pretends to be a bank official or a trusted person. They may ask the victim to share their OTP or UPI PIN, claiming it is needed for verification. In another method, the victim receives a payment request instead of money. If the user approves the request without checking, money gets deducted from their account.

Students and people who are not fully aware of digital payment systems are common targets. The fraud happens quickly, and victims often realize it only after losing money.

The consequences include financial loss, stress, and lack of trust in online transactions. In serious cases, victims may lose large amounts of money.

This case study highlights the importance of digital awareness and careful use of UPI and online payment platforms.
